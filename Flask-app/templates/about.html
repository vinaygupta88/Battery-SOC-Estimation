Bridging the gap between complex electrochemical dynamics and real-world Electric Vehicle (EV) performance using Multi-Sensor Fusion, Deep Learning, and Advanced Architectures.

The Motivation & The Challenge

The State of Charge (SOC) acts exactly like a fuel gauge in a gas-powered vehicle, indicating the remaining energy. However, accurately measuring SOC is a highly complex undertaking because it cannot be directly measured. It relies heavily on non-linear, time-varying electrochemical properties inside the battery.

Why traditional methods fail?

  • Coulomb Counting: Prone to error accumulation from current measurement errors, integration approximation, capacity uncertainty, and oscillator drift.
  • Kalman Filters: Highly accurate but computationally expensive and incredibly difficult to tune for dynamic conditions.

This project leverages Artificial Intelligence to automatically infer non-linear relationships, completely bypassing the need for solving complex analytical multiphysics equations.

Dataset & Feature Engineering

This project utilizes a robust, publicly available dataset from McMaster University. The experiments were performed on an LG HG2 18650 cell (Nominal Capacity: 3000 mAh, Voltage: 3.7V).

Thermal Testing

Tests were conducted across a wide ambient temperature range starting from 40°C all the way down to -20°C to capture thermal battery behaviors.

Drive Cycles

Data was simulated over various real-world EV drive cycles including UDDS, LA-92, US-06, and Mixed Cycles.

Preprocessing

We discovered that applying Min-Max Normalization drastically decreased scale distortion, easily outperforming standard Mean Normalization.

Machine Learning Architectures

Three powerful neural network architectures were trained, tuned, and evaluated to map time-series sensor telemetry to the continuous SOC prediction label.

Deep Neural Network (DNN)

Utilized multiple fully connected hidden layers (64 units each) with a ReLU activation function. The model processed 1 Hz resampled data and achieved solid convergence within 25 epochs.

Conv. Neural Network (CNN)

Using 1D Convolutional layers to extract local spatial features from voltage/current streams. It proved to be the most efficient, training in just 10-15 minutes with the lowest errors.

LSTM Networks

Built with 4 hidden layers and a SELU activation function. Capable of handling temporal dependencies natively. However, training required over 10 hours of heavy GPU compute.

Mathematical Formulation & Metrics

The performance of the predictive AI models is benchmarked against standard physical representations and statistical metrics.

Traditional Coulomb Counting

$$SOC(t) = SOC(t_0) - \frac{1}{C_n} \int_{t_0}^{t} I(\tau) d\tau$$

Where \( C_n \) is the nominal capacity and \( I(\tau) \) represents the discharge current over time. It demonstrates why integration errors accumulate heavily over long EV trips.

Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

$$MAE = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} |y_i - \hat{y}_i|$$

The primary loss function optimized during Backpropagation. It measures the absolute distance between actual SOC (\( y_i \)) and predicted SOC (\( \hat{y}_i \)).

Project Workflow

1️⃣ Data Collection

Battery telemetry including voltage, current, temperature and SOC labels were collected from the dataset.

2️⃣ Data Preprocessing

Noise removal, resampling and Min-Max normalization were applied to improve model convergence.

3️⃣ Model Training

DNN, CNN and LSTM architectures were trained to learn complex battery dynamics.

4️⃣ Model Evaluation

Models were evaluated using MAE metric to determine the most accurate architecture.

5️⃣ Deployment

The best model was integrated into a Flask web application for real-time SOC prediction.

Future Scope & Explainable AI

  • Mitigating Overfitting: Implementing advanced Regularization and Dropout techniques to further reduce the gap between training and validation loss.
  • Automated Hyperparameter Tuning: Utilizing TensorFlow's auto-tuning libraries to dynamically discover optimized neural network topologies.
  • Explainable AI (XAI): Integrating techniques like InputXGradient and Pairwise Importance Estimate Extension (PIEE) to break the "black-box" nature of models, allowing engineers to understand exactly which sensor parameters dictate battery degradation.
